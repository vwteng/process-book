The Process Book

Vivian Teng

INFO 360 Autumn 2013

The Beginning

This process book is a documentation of the development of Welp during the fall of 2013.

Brainstorming

Our first activity was to come up with possible design problems for our project. Since the project had to relate to problems college students faced, we came up with ideas based on our own experience. After we each created a list and combined our ideas, we saw a lot of ideas overlap. There were 5 areas we came up with; food, sleep, commute, finance and majors. Our group decided food would be the most interesting.

Ideating

We began to define the problem with "food". Based upon our own experience, we understood the difficulty of deciding where to eat. I avoid eating out because I end up at the same place every time. I try to find new places by asking my friends, but they are in the same situation as myself. We also came up with a list of consequences and causes of the problem.

Teammates

Teams consisted of three people. I worked with Willie and Patrick. When we first met, we shared our strengths based on the skills survey we filled out. We elected Willie as our team manager. Patrick was in charge of the technical aspect of our project. I took the role of the graphic designer and technical writer.

We organized a Facebook chat where we worked on our project together. Although we each had our own specialty, we worked together really well and each contributed to the assignments equally.

Interviews

In addition to working with my team, I conducted interviews with two interviewees. These interviews were meant to help us understand our problem by hearing people's experience with our deisgn problem. My group came up with a list of questions to guide our interviews. I interviewed Jenny and Kevin to see what their current eating habits were. I found out how often they ate on the Ave, where they ate and how they decide where to eat. The interviews confirmed the difficulties of finding restaurants to eat at.
